Mapping the Invisible: Missing and Dead Migrants

Overview Motivation Data Source Maps Clustering KDE LISA Evaluation Conclusion

Overview

This dashboard visualizes migrant deaths and disappearances along migration routes, focusing on the Mediterranean and global perspectives. The aim is to uncover spatial patterns and support humanitarian insights using interactive maps and spatial analysis methods.

Motivation

In Vienna, political campaigns often dominate public spaces, frequently with divisive, anti-migrant slogans. Yet behind these slogans are real people displaced by war, persecution, poverty, or climate disasters. No one willingly risks their life at sea unless the alternative is worse. This project aims to shift the focus back to the human cost of migration—through data, maps, and critical reflection.

Data Source

The data is provided by the International Organization for Migration (IOM) through the Missing Migrants Project. It documents deaths and disappearances during migration worldwide, including time, location, demographics, and cause of death. The dataset spans from 2014 to the present and is continuously updated by NGOs, media, and official sources.

Kernel Density Estimation (KDE)

The KDE map visualizes areas with a high concentration of migrant death incidents by estimating spatial density. This method emphasizes regions of intense risk and helps highlight critical zones in the Mediterranean Sea.

Local Indicators of Spatial Association (LISA)

LISA analysis identifies statistically significant clusters and spatial outliers. It provides insights into the spatial autocorrelation of incident locations—helping to understand whether high or low values cluster together more than would be expected by chance.

Evaluation & Interpretation

Our spatial techniques confirmed the intensity and structure of fatal migration events. The clustering results reveal clear migration corridors and highlight high-risk areas, especially in the Central Mediterranean. These patterns suggest systemic routes and dangers that are consistent over time, reinforcing the urgency for targeted humanitarian intervention.

Conclusion

Migrant deaths are not isolated tragedies, but part of broader systemic patterns. Our data analysis tools help make these patterns visible. However, the human story behind each data point must remain central: families, hopes, and the pursuit of safety. Through this dashboard, we aim to contribute to awareness and empathy—by making visible what is often invisible.
