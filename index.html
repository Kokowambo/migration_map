Mapping the Invisible: Missing and Dead Migrants

Overview Motivation Data Source Maps Clustering KDE LISA Evaluation Conclusion

Overview

This dashboard visualizes migrant deaths and disappearances along migration routes. It focuses on the Mediterranean and global perspectives. The aim is to uncover spatial patterns and support humanitarian insights using interactive maps and spatial analysis methods.

Motivation

In Vienna, political campaigns often dominate public spaces. Many of them use divisive and anti-migrant slogans. Behind these slogans are real people who have been displaced by war, persecution, poverty or climate disasters. In our opinion, people do not risk their life at sea unless the situation they leave behind is even worse. This project aims to shift the focus back to the human cost of migration by using data, maps and thoughtful reflection.

Data Source

The data is provided by the International Organization for Migration (IOM) through the Missing Migrants Project. It documents deaths and disappearances during migration worldwide. The dataset includes time, location, demographics and cause of death. It spans from 2014 to the present and is continuously updated by NGOs, media and official sources.

Heatmaps of Incidents

Global Heatmap of Missing and Dead Migrants

This map provides an overview of where migrant deaths have occurred around the world. It highlights major global routes where fatalities have been documented and helps identify patterns beyond the Mediterranean region.

Mediterranean Heatmap of Missing and Dead Migrants

This map focuses on the Mediterranean Sea, which is one of the most dangerous migration routes. The heatmap shows where the highest concentrations of fatal incidents occurred in this region.

Average Incident Locations by Month and Year

This visualization shows the average locations of incidents by month and by year. It helps to identify seasonal trends and how migration patterns may have shifted over time.

Clustering of Mediterranean Incidents

Clustering methods help us understand how migration-related deaths are grouped spatially. These methods reveal concentrations of risk and allow us to compare different algorithmic approaches.

Elbow Method

The elbow method helps determine the optimal number of clusters for KMeans. It plots the within-cluster sum of squares to find a balance between simplicity and accuracy.

KMeans Clustering (k=3)

This map shows the result of applying KMeans clustering with three clusters. It identifies major hotspots where incidents are spatially grouped.

DBSCAN Clustering

The DBSCAN method groups incidents based on spatial density. It is useful for detecting irregular cluster shapes and identifying noise or outliers.

Kernel Density Estimation (KDE)

The KDE map visualizes areas with a high concentration of migrant death incidents. It uses a density estimation method that helps to highlight critical zones in the Mediterranean Sea where risks are especially high.

Local Indicators of Spatial Association (LISA)

LISA analysis shows statistically significant clusters and spatial outliers. It gives insights into the spatial relationship of incident locations. The goal is to understand whether high or low values occur near each other more often than expected by chance.

Evaluation and Interpretation

Our spatial techniques confirmed the intensity and structure of fatal migration events. The clustering results reveal clear migration corridors and highlight high-risk areas, especially in the Central Mediterranean. These patterns suggest that certain routes are consistently more dangerous, which underlines the need for focused humanitarian responses.

Conclusion

Migrant deaths are not isolated events. They reflect broader and persistent problems. Our data analysis tools help to make these patterns visible. At the same time, it is important to remember that each data point represents a person with a story, a family and a hope for a better future. We hope this dashboard contributes to understanding and compassion by showing what is often hidden from view.
